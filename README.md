## 2. Project Description 

#### Team Name: Gradly

#### Project Topic Area:
- This platform is an intelligent course planning tool designed to help university students map out their entire academic journey. It combines semester-by-semester scheduling with insights such as GPA averages, professor options, and personalized pathways, making it easier to stay on track toward graduation. With collaborative and social features, it not only simplifies planning but also connects students with peers, study groups, and mentoring opportunities.

## 5. ABET Concerns Essay

Our Intelligent Course Planning Platform is influenced by several key constraints that shape 
its design, implementation, and deployment. Economic constraints play a major role, as 
we are developing this project with no dedicated funding and must rely on open-source 
tools, university-provided computing resources, and free-tier cloud services. This limits 
access to premium APIs or commercial-grade hosting, requiring careful optimization to 
ensure scalability and performance without incurring costs. 
Ethical constraints guide how we handle student information, as our system may deal with 
sensitive academic data such as GPA, grades, and course histories. We must ensure that 
all information is stored and processed responsibly, maintaining user consent and 
protecting privacy. Moreover, our recommendation algorithms must be designed to avoid 
bias or unfair academic advice that could negatively affect studentsâ€™ decisions. 
Security constraints are central to our design. Since the platform potentially stores student 
profiles and academic progress data, strong authentication, encrypted storage, and secure 
communication channels are essential. We must safeguard against unauthorized access, 
data breaches, or manipulation of course planning data. 
Finally, social constraints influence our platformâ€™s purpose and accessibility. Our goal is to 
create a system that benefits all students by promoting equitable academic support and 
peer collaboration. The tool must be inclusive, easy to use, and adaptable to diverse 
academic paths and student backgrounds. By enabling smarter planning and connections 
between students, advisors, and mentors, the project aims to positively impact the 
university learning experience.
